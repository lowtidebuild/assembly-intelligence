## Problem Statement

Korean organizations with government relations needs currently rely on a dedicated GR/PA person to manually compile legislative updates — checking National Assembly websites, filtering relevant bills, and distributing reports to stakeholders. This is expensive (dedicated salary), fragile (single point of failure when that person is unavailable), and slow (information arrives hours or days after legislative events occur).

There is no Korean equivalent of Quorum or FiscalNote. The market for AI-powered legislative intelligence in Korea is empty, while US equivalents command $10K-100K+/year per seat.

assembly-api-mcp provides 276 National Assembly Open APIs via MCP server, making real-time programmatic access to all legislative data freely available. The opportunity: build a polished internal intelligence dashboard that makes the GR/PA person 10x more effective by combining automated tracking, LLM-powered semantic analysis, and professional visualization.

## Demand Evidence

- A dedicated GR/PA person exists inside the organization whose core job includes legislative monitoring
- This person manually compiles and distributes legislative updates to stakeholders
- The organization already allocates real salary and hours to this problem
- The user identified this person specifically (not a hypothetical category)
- Demand started as hypothesis but upgraded to observed internal process during session

## Status Quo

Internal person/team manually compiles and shares legislative updates:
- Checks National Assembly websites (의안정보시스템, 국민참여입법센터) daily
- Filters for industry-relevant bills, schedules, and votes
- Creates summary reports and distributes via email/internal channels
- Tracks bill lifecycle status manually
- Monitors specific legislators' activity when relevant

Cost: dedicated GR/PA salary + hours/week on compilation. Risk: information gap when person is unavailable.

## Target User & Product Vision

**Primary user:** The dedicated GR/PA person — their daily tool, not a replacement for their judgment.

**Secondary users:** Executives and stakeholders who receive the GR/PA person's reports (the dashboard gives them self-serve access).

**Product vision:** A unified "산업별 국회 인텔리전스 대시보드" with four integrated components:

### A. 입법 레이더 (Legislative Radar)
Industry keyword/profile configuration → automated full lifecycle tracking of relevant bills. 입법예고 → 발의 → 심사 → 표결 timeline visualization. Daily cron detects and alerts on status changes.

### B. 의원 워치 (Legislator Watch)
Comprehensive activity analysis of industry-relevant legislators: sponsored bills, voting patterns, committee memberships, speech records. Enables the GR/PA person to brief stakeholders on key legislators instantly.

### C. 국회 브리핑봇 (Assembly Briefing Bot)
Daily auto-generated one-page briefing: "오늘 국회에서 우리 산업 관련 뭐가 있나." Combines schedule + pending bills + recent votes + legislation notices into a single actionable summary. Delivered before the GR/PA person starts work.

### D. 법안 영향 분석기 (Bill Impact Analyzer)
On-demand AI-generated deep analysis of any specific bill: 제안이유, 주요내용, 심사현황, 관련 의원, 유사 과거 법안, 과거 유사법안 표결 패턴 분석. Transforms raw legislative data into executive-ready analysis. (Note: "표결 전망" is replaced with historical voting pattern analysis — predictive forecasting would be speculative and could undermine trust.)

### E. 관련 뉴스 (Related News)
For each high-relevance bill (score 3+), auto-search recent news articles mentioning the bill name, key terms, or related legislators. Displayed as a sidebar panel on bill detail pages and included in daily briefings. Provides political context, industry reactions, and public sentiment that formal Assembly data alone cannot capture. Uses web search API (Google Custom Search or SerpAPI) to fetch Korean news results.

## Constraints

- Internal tool for one organization (no multi-tenancy needed initially)
- Must integrate with assembly-api-mcp as primary data backend, supplemented by web search for news context
- Korean language UI and content throughout
- Polished, professional frontend design is critical — ParlaWatch+ aesthetic (professional gov-tech dashboard). Design quality signals seriousness to decision-makers and earns continued investment.
- Must be demo-able to leadership for internal greenlight
- The GR/PA person must find it useful on Day 1, not after weeks of configuration

## Premises

1. **Augment, don't replace.** The tool augments the GR/PA person's judgment — it surfaces information faster, but the human decides what matters and how to act.
2. **assembly-api-mcp is the primary data source**, supplemented by web search for news context. The 276 APIs cover all formal legislative data. News search adds political context and public sentiment that formal data cannot capture.
3. **Ship the full dashboard from day one.** All four components are views on the same data source. The marginal cost of building all four is low, and phasing adds coordination overhead without meaningful risk reduction. (Note: challenged by second opinion — see below.)
4. **LLM semantic relevance scoring is a key capability.** Reading full 제안이유 and determining industry relevance semantically (not just keyword matching) is what makes this tool dramatically better than checking 의안정보시스템 manually.
5. **Internal tool first.** Productization for external organizations is deferred. Build for one org, one industry, one GR/PA person.

## Cross-Model Perspective

An independent Claude subagent reviewed the session context cold. Key findings:

**Steelman:** "A vertical AI intelligence layer for Korean government relations — the equivalent of building Bloomberg Terminal for Korean legislative tracking, starting as an internal tool where the feedback loop is tight."

**Key insight:** The push to build all four components at once is "simultaneously correct and dangerous." Correct architecturally (shared data model), wrong as product strategy (you phase to reduce validation risk, not technical risk). Predicted that Briefing Bot will get 80%+ of daily usage while Legislator Watch barely gets used.

**Prototype recommendation:** Component C only (Briefing Bot). Python, Claude API, Slack webhook. No UI. Test one hypothesis: does LLM semantic relevance on Korean legislative text beat manual monitoring?

**User's response:** Defended full dashboard vision twice — against both the primary advisor and the second opinion. Internal context (greenlight demo) requires visual impact.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Daily Build" — Static Dashboard + Cron
Cron job runs daily, calls assembly-api-mcp, generates a self-contained HTML dashboard with all 4 views. No database, no server.
- Effort: S (~2 hours with CC)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Zero infrastructure, easy to demo, self-contained
- Cons: Not real-time, no interactivity, no drill-down

### Approach B: "Full Stack" — Next.js + DB + Real-time (CHOSEN)
Proper web application with Next.js frontend, database for tracking state, scheduled sync from assembly-api-mcp, push notifications via Slack/email.
- Effort: M (human: ~1 week / CC: ~2-3 days for polished MVP)
- Risk: Medium
- Pros: Interactive drill-down, real-time alerts, multi-user ready, most impressive demo
- Cons: Needs hosting and database, more maintenance

### Approach C: "AI-Native" — Conversational Interface
No traditional dashboard. AI chat interface connected to assembly-api-mcp. Auto-briefings to Slack/email. On-demand natural language queries.
- Effort: S (~1.5 hours with CC)
- Risk: Low-Med
- Pros: Fastest to ship, most natural UX, easiest to iterate
- Cons: Harder to demo to leadership, no visual dashboard

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Full Stack** — Next.js web application with polished, professional UI.

### Key Design Decisions

1. **MCP integration method:** assembly-api-mcp exposes an SSE-based MCP endpoint (`https://assembly-api-mcp.fly.dev/mcp?key=YOUR_API_KEY&profile=lite`). The dashboard uses an MCP client SDK (e.g., `@modelcontextprotocol/sdk`) to call MCP tools like `search_bills`, `get_votes`, `get_schedule`, etc. Not plain REST — MCP protocol over SSE transport.

2. **Relevance scoring pipeline:** On each sync, new/changed bills pass through Gemini API with the organization's industry profile. The LLM reads the full 제안이유 and scores relevance 1-5. Only bills scoring 3+ appear in default views.
   - **Prompt structure:** System prompt includes the IndustryProfile description + keywords. User message contains bill title + 제안이유 + 주요내용. Response: JSON `{ score: 1-5, reasoning: "..." }`.
   - **Scale:** Typical active session has ~50-200 new/changed bills per week. Average 제안이유 is ~500-2000 tokens. Estimated cost: ~$0.10-0.50/day using Gemini Flash for screening (score only), Gemini Pro for deep analysis (Component D). Gemini's Korean language support is strong and pricing is significantly lower than alternatives.
   - **Tiered model:** Gemini Flash for daily relevance screening (fast, very cheap). Gemini Pro for on-demand bill impact analysis and briefing generation (higher quality).
   - **Feedback loop:** GR/PA person can override relevance scores via dashboard toggle (thumbs up/down). Overrides are stored and used to refine the industry profile prompt over time.

3. **Daily briefing generation:** Cron runs at 21:30 UTC (= 06:30 KST next day). Collects yesterday's changes, today's schedule, pending high-relevance bills. Gemini Pro generates a one-page Korean briefing. Available on dashboard (브리핑봇 page). No Slack delivery for MVP (Slack API unavailable internally).

4. **Frontend design priority:** ParlaWatch visual language as the base, extended for 5-component dashboard. Light theme default with dark mode toggle. Target aesthetic: ParlaWatch+ (professional gov-tech dashboard, breathable spacing, not Bloomberg density).
   - **Color (light):** #f8fafc bg, #ffffff surface, #2563eb primary, #3b82f6 domain accent, #e2e8f0 border. Status colors: amber (심사중), green (가결), red (부결), gray (폐기/입법예고)
   - **Color (dark):** #1a1d21 bg, #222529 surface, #1d9bd1 primary, #383a3e border
   - **Typography:** System fonts (-apple-system, 'Pretendard' fallback), 14px base, line-height 1.6
   - **Layout:** Variant C — 240px left sidebar navigation (fixed, sticky) + 2-column workspace (main content left, context widgets right). Brand "산업별 국회 인텔리전스" in sidebar.
   - **Navigation:** Sidebar with 5 items: 브리핑봇, 입법 레이더, 의원 워치, 영향 분석기, 설정. Active item: blue left-bar indicator + light blue background.
   - **Components:** Sortable/filterable data tables (입법 레이더 primary view), card grids (브리핑봇), slide-over detail panel (500px right), editable text fields (당사 영향 사항), skeleton loaders (shimmer rows).
   - **Card style:** White surface, 1px #e2e8f0 border, 8px radius, left 4px border-left #3b82f6 for domain-relevant items. ParlaWatch pattern.
   - **Responsive:** 브리핑봇 only responsive (sidebar → hamburger, cards stack). 입법 레이더 table = desktop only.
   - **Approved mockup:** ~/.gstack/projects/assembly-intelligence/designs/briefing-bot-20260408/variant-C.png

5. **Database choice tied to hosting:** SQLite (via Drizzle ORM) for local/Docker deployment. If Vercel is chosen, use PostgreSQL (Neon or Supabase) instead — SQLite is not compatible with Vercel's ephemeral filesystem.

6. **Error handling and resilience:**
   - API calls to assembly-api-mcp: 3 retries with exponential backoff (1s, 3s, 9s)
   - If MCP server is unavailable during sync: serve stale data with visible "Last synced: X hours ago" red banner at top of main content
   - If sync job fails: red banner on dashboard ("동기화 실패 — 마지막 데이터 표시 중"). No Slack alert for MVP.
   - All synced data cached locally in the database — dashboard remains functional with stale data during outages
   - **Cold start UX:** Neon DB cold start (2-3s after 5min idle) → show skeleton loader (shimmer table rows) until data loads. Sidebar sync indicator ("06:30 동기화 완료") reassures user that data is fresh, just loading.

7. **Data sync strategy:** Two sync cycles per day (Vercel Hobby compatible):
   - **Morning sync (06:30 KST / 21:30 UTC previous day):** Full data collection. New/changed bills → keyword pre-filter → Gemini Flash relevance scoring → Bill.summary_text generation. Daily briefing generation.
   - **Evening sync (18:30 KST / 09:30 UTC):** Change detection only. Checks bill status transitions since morning sync. Updates stage/status columns. Captures afternoon legislative activity.
   - Initial load: current National Assembly session data only. Subsequent syncs are incremental, fetching updates since `last_synced` timestamp.
   - All MCP calls are server-side only (Next.js API routes). The API key is never exposed to the browser. For Vercel hosting, use short-lived request-response patterns via server actions — avoid long-running SSE connections in serverless functions.

8. **Change detection & in-app alerts:** When a bill with `relevance_score >= 3` transitions between lifecycle stages (입법예고 → 발의 → 위원회회부 → 위원회심사 → 법사위체계자구심사 → 본회의부의 → 본회의의결), the change is logged and highlighted on the dashboard (브리핑봇 "오늘의 핵심" section + 입법 레이더 status column). No external alert delivery for MVP (Slack API unavailable). External notifications deferred to post-greenlight.

9. **Component quality tiers for Day 1:** All four components ship, but at different polish levels:
   - **Production quality:** Component C (브리핑봇) — this is the daily driver
   - **Full interactive:** Component A (입법레이더) — bill tracking with drill-down
   - **Demo quality:** Component B (의원워치) + Component D (영향분석기) — real data, basic UI, limited interactivity. Polished post-greenlight based on usage data.

10. **Industry profile setup:** On first run, a setup wizard collects: organization name, industry sector (dropdown), 3-5 industry keywords, and a 2-3 sentence description of what legislation matters. Stored in IndustryProfile table. Can be edited via Settings page. No ongoing configuration needed — the LLM uses this profile for all relevance scoring.

11. **Authentication:** Simple password gate (shared team password, no individual accounts) for MVP. Upgrade to SSO/OAuth post-greenlight if the tool expands to more users.

### 13. 당사 영향 사항 (Company Impact Assessment)

The most important field missing from the original design. This is where the GR/PA person writes their professional judgment about how a bill affects the company.

**Two modes (toggle):**
1. **직접 작성 (Manual):** Empty text field. GR/PA types their assessment directly. Default mode.
2. **AI 초안 (AI Draft):** Button generates a draft using Gemini Pro based on bill's 제안이유 + 주요내용 + industry profile. Shows with "AI 초안 (미확인)" label. GR/PA edits and confirms. Confirmed drafts remove the label.

**Where it appears:**
- 입법 레이더 table: truncated column (first line visible)
- Slide-over detail panel: full editable text area
- Daily briefing (브리핑봇): included for high-relevance bills (score 4-5)

**Not AI-generated by default.** The tool augments the GR/PA person's judgment, it does not replace it. An unconfirmed AI draft should be visually distinct (muted text + "AI 초안" badge).
